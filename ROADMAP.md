# LEBANESE ACADEMIC — 18-Month Roadmap

*The long view. Where this site is going if it actually becomes what it could be.*

---

## The thesis behind the roadmap

A publication does not become serious by *looking* serious. It becomes serious by surviving. The single number that determines whether Lebanese Academic exists in 2027 is **content velocity** — how many essays you ship per month, sustained, over 18 months. Everything else (design, Arabic, SEO, partnerships) is a multiplier on that number. A zero multiplied by a multiplier is still zero.

So this roadmap is built around content cadence, with everything else slotting in around it.

## The three honest paths

There are three structurally different versions of Lebanese Academic you could ship. They are not on a spectrum — they are different *kinds of object*. Pick one before chasing the other two.

### Path 1 — The personal essay site (low friction, high integrity)

You as a single voice. One essay every 10–14 days. No outside writers. No submissions inbox. The site is a slow-publishing front for your thinking, fed by Instagram. The advantage is honesty — you can sustain this for years without burning out, and the IG-to-site funnel works without an editorial pipeline. The cost is that you cap at 30k–40k regular readers and the site never becomes a venue other writers want to be in.

Cadence: ~3 essays/month. Total at month 18: ~70 essays. Headcount: 1 (you). Cost: $0 plus your time.

### Path 2 — The independent literary-political quarterly (high friction, high upside)

You as founder/editor, with a curated bench of 4–6 outside writers contributing 1–2 essays each per quarter. Real editorial calendar. Real submissions process. Real (small) payments to outside writers. This is *Megaphone* or *The Public Source* at a similar stage. The site becomes a place serious Lebanese intellectuals want to be associated with.

Cadence: ~6 essays/month (4 from you, 2 from outside). Total at month 18: ~110 essays. Headcount: 1 + 4–6 contracted writers + maybe 1 part-time editor. Cost: ~$500–1,500/month for writer payments + hosting + Resend + minor tooling.

### Path 3 — The weekly publication of record (very high friction, transformational upside)

You as editor-in-chief of a real small magazine with a paid bench, weekly publishing rhythm, and ambitions toward print issues. This is the path that ends in a Substack-class business with paying subscribers or a foundation-funded nonprofit.

Cadence: 4 essays/week + letters + notebook. Total at month 18: 200+ essays. Headcount: 1 + 8–12 writers + 1 full-time editor + part-time copy editor. Cost: $3k–10k/month sustained.

## My recommendation for KINGO specifically

**Path 2.** Here is why.

Path 1 wastes the 40k IG followers — they are too many for a personal site and you will feel the audience-channel mismatch as a slow leak of attention. Path 3 is premature — you don't have the editorial muscle yet to filter quality at weekly cadence, you'll publish thinner work to make the rhythm, and the brand will collapse. Path 2 is the only path that matches your actual current assets (one strong voice, a clear visual identity, a real research corpus, a distribution channel with 40k followers, no team yet).

The structural argument: Path 2 is the only path where the *next* publication move (going weekly, going paid, hiring) is a natural step rather than a quantum leap. You can ship Path 2 for 12 months and then choose to graduate to Path 3 if the numbers earn it. Going straight to Path 3 is gambling.

  - **M3** — First two outside-writer commissions sent. Target writers: one academic Lebanese voice (someone from AUB, LAU, or USJ political science with a real argument), one diaspora voice (Paris, Montreal, or Dubai-based intellectual who reads serious magazines). Pay $200–400 per essay.
  - **M3** — Newsletter live, weekly cadence. First 500 subscribers from IG.

End-of-phase target: 22 essays in archive, 1 in Arabic, 2 by outside writers, weekly newsletter to 500+, About page polished, no thin sketches in the public archive.

### Phase II — Months 4–9 — Build the bench

This phase exists to prove that Path 2 can be sustained. The single hardest move is going from "founder writing alone" to "founder editing a team of 4–6." Most independent publications die in exactly this transition.

  - **M4–M6** — Sign 4 outside writers to a *commitment letter* (not a contract — too heavy at this stage) for 1 essay per quarter. Pay rate fixed at $300/essay until M9 review. Each writer is given a beat: South Lebanon politics, banking and finance, Beirut urbanism, Arab-language press translation/commentary.
  - **M4–M6** — Develop a *house style guide* (1,500–2,500 word essays with mandatory thesis-in-first-paragraph, named institutions and dates, no manifestos, structural framing). This is non-negotiable infrastructure. Without it, you'll spend forever editing.
  - **M5** — Move newsletter from Resend transactional to a real ESP with proper analytics. ConvertKit/Beehiiv/Substack. Decide before M5.
  - **M6** — Second Arabic essay. Third. Building the Arabic side from one piece to four pieces by M9.
  - **M7** — Pitch to 3 podcasts. The publication should be appearing on the podcasts your audience listens to. *The Beirut Banyan*, *The Lebanese Politics Podcast*, *Lawdable* — all reasonable starts. Each appearance is a 5x newsletter subscriber bump if done well.
  - **M8** — Decision point: are you publishing 5–6 essays per month consistently? If yes, proceed to Phase III. If no, *do not advance* — fix the cadence problem before adding anything.
  - **M9** — Half-year audit. Re-read every essay shipped. Decide which 2–3 should be republished as the "first issue" in a curated PDF/print-style download — this is the moment to actually deliver on the *Issue 01* promise.

End-of-phase target: 50 essays in archive, 4 in Arabic, 12 by outside writers, newsletter to 2,500+, two podcast appearances, "Issue 01" curated PDF shipped to subscribers.

### Phase III — Months 10–18 — The transition decision

By M12 you will know whether Path 2 is structurally working. The signs are: outside writers pitching you unsolicited, journalists citing your essays, the newsletter doubling organically, paid newsletter subscribers (Stripe/Memberful) reaching 100+ at $5–8/month.

If those signs are present:

  - **M10** — Introduce a paid newsletter tier. $6/month or $60/year. Paid subs get: early access to essays, the monthly compiled PDF, a quarterly editor's letter, and a private community (Discord/Substack chat). Target: 200 paid subs by M12 ($1,200–1,400 MRR — pays the writers).
  - **M11** — First commissioned long-form investigation. 6,000–10,000 words. Months in the making. The kind of piece that gets cited by *L'Orient Today* or *Megaphone*. Budget: $1,500–3,000.
  - **M12** — Hire a part-time copy editor / managing editor. 10 hours/week. $400–600/month. This is the single hire that determines whether you make it to Path 3.
  - **M13–M18** — Stabilize at Path 2 cadence. Sign 2 more writers. First commissioned cover essay. First print run (50–100 copies) of a single curated essay collection, sold at $25/each at a Beirut bookshop launch event. This is your first physical artifact.

End-of-phase target: 110 essays in archive, 12 in Arabic, 35 by outside writers, newsletter at 6,000+ subscribers with 200+ paid, one print object in the world, one part-time editor hired.

## What kills you

These are the failure modes that destroy independent publications in this exact phase. Watch for them.

  - **The thin-archive death spiral.** You publish 5 essays in a month, then 2, then 1. Each missed week is a small signal to the audience that you are not committed. By month 6 you have an archive that looks like an enthusiastic start that fizzled. *Cadence is the brand.* Pick a cadence you can defend in your worst week and stick to it.
  - **The founder-bottleneck collapse.** You edit every outside writer's piece five times because none of them is quite right. By M9 you have rewritten more outside writing than your own, the writers feel managed not edited, and you burn out simultaneously editing and writing. *Solution: house style guide so strict that 70% of an outside piece comes back to you already in voice.*
  - **The vibes-without-substance trap.** The site keeps looking better — more typography, more chrome, more Arabic — but the actual essays don't get more rigorous. By M12 you have a beautiful publication that serious readers smell as hollow. *Solution: every design improvement must be accompanied by an editorial improvement on the same day. No exceptions.*
  - **The Instagram funnel becoming the audience.** The IG audience is broad, casual, and conditioned on visual hooks. If you optimize the site for them you will dumb down the writing. *Solution: explicitly target the small, hard-to-reach audience — diaspora intellectuals, academics, journalists — and accept that the IG funnel delivers maybe 1% of its followers to the site. That 1% is the real audience. 400 serious readers > 40k casual ones.*
  - **The partisan ammunition drift.** As the audience grows, you'll get pressure (subtle, then explicit) to take sides — pro-resistance, anti-resistance, pro-Saudi, pro-Iran, pro-March-14, pro-March-8. The minute you accept that frame, you lose the cross-sectarian intellectual reader who was your real audience. *Solution: explicit non-partisan policy in the About page (we already added it). Defend it editorially when challenged.*

## The metrics that actually matter

Forget IG followers. Track these:

  - **Cadence consistency** — essays per month, 6-month moving average. Target by M6: 4. By M12: 6. Standard deviation matters more than the mean.
  - **Outside writer ratio** — % of essays not by you. Target by M6: 10%. By M12: 30%. By M18: 50%.
  - **Repeat readership** — % of newsletter opens that come back week-on-week. Target: 60%.
  - **Forward rate** — newsletter forwards per send. Target: 5%. Hard to measure but you'll feel it.
  - **Citation rate** — number of times your essays are linked from *L'Orient Today*, *Megaphone*, *Daraj*, *The Public Source*, or major Anglophone outlets per quarter. Target by M12: 4.

The metric that matters more than any of these: *are you proud of the last 5 essays you shipped?* If yes, the rest follows. If no, nothing else fixes it.
