## Blunt Diagnosis

The website has a strong identity, but it is not yet operating like a serious editorial website. The problem is not one font, one image, or one page. The problem is that the site has pieces of a beautiful magazine, pieces of an archive, pieces of a blog, and pieces of a submission platform, but they are not yet fused into one reading system.

The essays are the core asset. Right now, the essay template does not honor them enough. The page opens like a dressed-up document: giant title, byline, then a long text column. There is too little pacing, too little orientation, too little visual intelligence, and too little editorial infrastructure around the piece.

If I were being paid serious money to make this excellent, I would rebuild the site around one rule:

> Every page should make the reader feel they have entered a serious Lebanese editorial world, not clicked into a pretty file.

## Non-Negotiable Direction

1. Make the essay reading page the most important page on the site.
2. Treat the homepage as a front page, not a poster wall.
3. Turn the essays page into a map of the project, not a tag dump.
4. Build real pages for letters and notebook entries.
