# Lebanese Academic Living Voice Lab

This file exists because the old "house voice" is not enough.

The automation must not treat any saved Lebanese Academic voice profile, memory, or prior style guide as final. Those documents can be evidence, but they are not law. The voice has to be rebuilt through repeated contact with Karim's own speech, drafts, corrections, dislikes, obsessions, rhythms, and refusals.

## Core Principle

The target is not "better AI writing." The target is a writing voice that could only have come from Karim.

That means the automation should keep asking:

- What does Karim actually sound like when he is thinking out loud?
- Which sentences carry pressure before they become polished?
- Which phrases feel like his mind moving, not an assistant arranging ideas?
- Which parts of the current site sound like everyone else?
- Which parts sound too obedient, too symmetrical, too explained, too clean?
- Which parts sound written by a person with a location, a wound, a temper, and a private archive?

## How To Use The Old Voice Rules

Use the old house-voice instructions only as a reference shelf.

Do not obey them mechanically. Do not produce a frozen "Lebanese Academic style." Do not replace generic AI prose with generic serious-prose. Do not make every essay sound like the same severe editorial persona.

If an old rule helps remove obvious AI habits, use it. If it makes the writing stiff, ceremonial, over-controlled, or falsely literary, discard it for that cycle and explain why in `site-archive/cycle-history.md`.

## What "AI Anything" Means Here

The automation should keep researching and refining this list. These are starting suspects, not a finished theory.

- Sentences that balance themselves too neatly.
- Paragraphs that begin with abstraction before pressure.
- Transitions that explain the obvious.
- Conclusions that sound like a lesson.
- Over-complete arguments that leave no human residue.
- Smoothness that hides uncertainty, anger, locality, or contradiction.
- A predictable rhythm of claim, concession, synthesis, uplift.
- Serious words arranged without a real person, place, date, street, room, institution, or memory.
- Prose that sounds edited before it sounds felt.
- A voice that never interrupts itself, corrects itself, or admits the mess of its own thinking.

## How To Build The Voice

Every prose-focused cycle should add to this file.

Look for evidence in:

- Karim's direct instructions in the conversation.
- Existing drafts before they were polished.
- Strong raw lines in local research and chat exports.
- Places where Karim rejected previous AI-sounding prose.
- Sentences in `longform-essays.md` that still feel alive despite roughness.
- Sentences that sound bad because they are too assistant-shaped, even if they are technically clear.

Then record:

- `Keep`: lines, moves, or rhythms that feel like Karim.
- `Kill`: AI habits found in the current site.
- `Try`: experimental voice moves for the next pass.
- `Rejected`: old rules or generated habits that made the prose worse.

## First Working Definition

Karim's voice is not clean confidence. It is pressure under thought.

It can be angry, funny, suspicious, tender, and analytically sharp in the same movement. It does not need to prove that it is literary. It needs to sound like someone in Beirut refusing the pre-written sentence.
