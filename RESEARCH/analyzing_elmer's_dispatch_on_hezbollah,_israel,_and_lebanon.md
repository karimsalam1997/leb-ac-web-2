## The structural argument first

Capital in Lebanon is not ideologically neutral and never has been. The commercial-financial fraction of the Christian elite — the bankers, the insurance holders, the import-export families, the real estate consolidators — has a **coherent class interest** that has been remarkably stable across a century of regime changes. That interest has three legs:

**One, dollarization as the instrument of wealth preservation.** Their capital is not really Lebanese. It is globally held, correspondent-banked through New York and Zurich, and its value is protected by maintaining access to the dollar-clearing system. Anything that threatens that access — sanctions exposure, FATF greylisting, association with designated entities — is an existential threat to them personally, not an abstract policy concern.

**Two, the Lebanese economy as a rent-extraction platform rather than a productive base.** Their business model has never been industrial. It has been intermediation — moving Gulf deposits, diaspora remittances, and international capital through Lebanese institutions and taking the spread. This requires a state weak enough to tax lightly but stable enough to clear transactions. A functional developmental state would be as threatening to them as a collapsed state, possibly more so.

**Three, physical security guaranteed by whoever is currently hegemonic.** In the 1950s the guarantor was Chamoun's pro-Western alignment. In the 1970s it was the Syrian entry. In the 2000s it was the post-Hariri Saudi-American axis. The Christian commercial elite has always understood that their villas in Achrafieh and their summer houses in Faqra sit on geography that requires a foreign umbrella. They have no ideological commitment to *which* umbrella, only to the principle that there must be one.

Given those three legs, **an alignment with the Israeli-American axis in 2026 is not a betrayal of Christian Lebanon. It is the logical next iteration of a pattern the class has executed repeatedly.** Sehnaoui is not an outlier. He is the class expressing itself in conditions of Hezbollah's military degradation and American regional ascendancy.
